So you want to start something?

Doesn't matter what it is - you've got plenty of resources available to help you out.

Olin and Babson Resources

  • Olin College Foundry
    • The Foundry is Olin's student entrepreneurship organization - and it's packed full of people who can help you with connections, advice, and experience with startups at Olin. Send a helpme, or email foundryboard@lists.olin.edu.
  • Butler Venture Accelerator
    • A program at Babson which provides work space, peer mentoring, workshops and speakers, and entrepreneurial community, a dseed fund, and expert advisors for students working on startups.
  • B.E.T.A. Challenge
    • A competition at Babson for businesses in the Babson + Olin community that have "achieved major milestones by taking action." The grand prize is $20,000.
  • Summer Venture Program
    • A 10-week intensive experience designed to accelerate the development of student entrepreneurial ventures; students are provided with housing, work space, advisors, a speaker series, and other resources.
  • Babson Social Innovation Lab
    • An action tank for social entrepreneurs dedicated to building a better world through responsible business.
  • Iterate
    • Iterate is one of Olin’s entrepreneurship elective courses. By design, it provides students with the resources to explore the entrepreneurial potential of their ideas and their ability to create value “in the wild.”
  • Launch
    • As one of Olin’s entrepreneurship elective courses, Launch supports students looking to apply to external sources such as business model competitions and venture capital for resources to advance their product, business, creative and impact ideas.
  • Keith Hopper, Olin Entrepreneur in Residence
    • Keith is a Lecturer of Entrepreneurship at Olin. He is a Mentor at Techstars, a global startup accelerator, where he helps startup companies with product strategy and execution, emphasizing Customer Development towards product-market fit. Keith is also co-founder and Trustee of the revolutionary micro-philanthropy, The Awesome Foundation, which forwards the interest of awesome in the universe through 89 chapters in 18 countries. Prior to this, Keith was the Director of Product Strategy and Development at NPR.
  • Lawrence Neeley, Olin Professor of Entrepreneurship
    • Dr. Lawrence Neeley joined the Olin faculty as an Assistant Professor of Design and Entrepreneurship in 2011. He brings to Olin his passion for design, prototyping, manufacturing and entrepreneurship; and strives to share these passions with his students and the Olin community. Both his research and educational efforts help designers rapidly imagine, realize and offer compelling real world products.

Venture Capital on Campus

  • Rough Draft Ventures
    • Rough Draft Ventures is a student team (yes, that means actual students!) which makes small investments ($25,000) in student companies. The funding decisions are made by Student Partners, which means that you personally know someone who can help you through the process.
  • .406 Ventures
    • .406 Ventures is a large tech VC firm, and their Student Fellows program builds upon the academic entrepreneurial experiences offered through school with the real-world skills and networks needed to start successful companies. Over two years in the program, each Fellow will build strong relationships with 15-20 other leading student entrepreneurs and experience the venture capital process.
  • Contrary Capital
    • Contrary Capital is a decentralized, university-focused venture capital fund with a laser-focus on empowering and investing in the brightest young minds. Funding decisions are made by a network of students across the country, which means that Contrary's Student Partner at Olin can help you find the funding you need.
  • MassChallenge
    • MassChallenge is a non-profit organization on a mission help startups win without taking equity. They give out over $2M in equity-free cash prizes globally every year to help high-impact and high-potential startups win.
